Q : What is the difference between OOP and POP? 
Ans: 

      POP (Procedure Oriented Programme)                OOP (Object Oriented Programme)

    - It is Top to Bottom Approach.                   - It is Bottom to Top Approach.

    - Data move freely in programme.                  - Data does not move freely in programme.

    - The length of program is more compare to        - The length of program is less compare to
      OOP.                                              POP.

    - There is no object.                             - Object can communicate with each other.

    - Programmes divided into function.               - Programmes divided into projects.

    - We can not secure data in POP.                  - We can secure data in OOP.
